GBM Failure Rate ?
Geometric Brownian Motion Failure
Probability of insolvency using a lognormal price model with constant drift and volatility. This is the baseline "smooth" model.
Why it matters: Provides a lower-bound risk estimate assuming no sudden crashes or regime changes.
—
Jump Diff Failure ?
Jump Diffusion Failure
Probability of insolvency using Merton's jump-diffusion model which adds random crash events to the baseline GBM model.
Why it matters: Captures tail risk from sudden BTC crashes like exchange failures or regulatory shocks.
—
Bootstrap Failure ?
Block Bootstrap Failure
Probability of insolvency by resampling actual historical BTC returns in blocks, preserving autocorrelation and volatility clustering.
Why it matters: Most realistic estimate—uses actual market behavior without distributional assumptions.
—
Regime Switch Failure ?
Regime Switching Bootstrap Failure
Probability of insolvency using a non-parametric model that samples from historical periods (regimes) to generate price paths, capturing shifts in market dynamics.
Why it matters: Provides a more realistic risk estimate by incorporating fat tails, volatility clustering, and regime shifts observed in real-world data, without assuming a fixed distribution.
